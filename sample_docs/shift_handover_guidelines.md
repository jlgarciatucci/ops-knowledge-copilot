# Shift Handover Guidelines

**Document ID:** OPS-HAND-001
**Version:** 1.0
**Department:** Operations
**Document Type:** Shift Handover Guideline
**Effective Date:** 2026-01-01
**Classification:** Synthetic demo document

## 1. Purpose

This guideline defines the minimum information that must be transferred between outgoing and incoming shift teams. The purpose is to prevent information loss, improve operational continuity and ensure that open actions, risks and abnormal conditions are understood.

## 2. Required Handover Information

Every shift handover must include current operational status, unavailable or restricted equipment, active alarms, recent trips, work permits active or pending closure, maintenance activities, safety observations, environmental concerns, open actions, expected events, required management notifications and deviations from normal procedures.

The outgoing shift supervisor is responsible for complete and accurate handover. The incoming shift supervisor must ask clarifying questions before accepting it.

## 3. Open Actions

Open actions must include action description, owner, priority, due time, current status, dependencies and escalation requirement if delayed. Actions with safety impact must be clearly marked. If an action cannot be completed during the shift, the reason must be recorded.

## 4. Operational Risks

The handover must identify operational risks that may affect the next shift. Risks include equipment near alarm limits, reduced redundancy, temporary manual operation, delayed maintenance, weather constraints, staffing limitations, pending isolation, unstable process conditions or communication issues.

For each risk, the outgoing team must explain what could happen, which asset is affected, what monitoring is required, what action should be taken if the condition worsens and who must be notified. High-risk items must be verbally confirmed by the incoming supervisor.

## 5. Equipment Status

Equipment status must be communicated as Available, Restricted, Standby, Out of Service, Under Maintenance or Testing. The handover must include all unavailable, restricted or recently returned equipment.

For equipment under maintenance, the handover must include work order number, isolation status, responsible technician, expected completion time and required validation before return to service.

## 6. Escalation Notes

Escalation notes must be included when the next shift may need management, safety, maintenance or IT support. The handover must state who has been informed, what decision is pending, what condition triggers escalation, which communication channel to use and whether the escalation is urgent.

Examples of escalation triggers include a critical alarm repeating more than three times, standby asset failing to start, safety barrier unavailable, open action delayed beyond agreed time or customer-impacting event.
