# Maintenance Procedure: Pump Station

**Document ID:** OPS-MNT-001
**Version:** 1.0
**Department:** Maintenance Operations
**Document Type:** Maintenance Procedure
**Effective Date:** 2026-01-01
**Classification:** Synthetic demo document

## 1. Purpose

This procedure defines the standard maintenance and operational response for a medium-voltage pump station used in industrial water circulation systems. The objective is to ensure that startup, abnormal vibration, overheating, emergency shutdown and post-maintenance validation are performed safely and consistently.

## 2. Startup Checks

Before starting the pump, the technician must confirm that the work permit is closed or transferred, guards are installed, lubrication levels are acceptable, cooling or ventilation is available, suction and discharge valves are correctly positioned, emergency stop is accessible, protection relay is reset and no active alarms are present.

If any startup condition is not satisfied, the pump must remain stopped and the issue must be recorded in the maintenance log.

## 3. Abnormal Vibration

If abnormal vibration occurs after startup, the technician must keep a safe distance, check vibration readings, compare values with the accepted baseline, inform the shift supervisor, reduce load if possible and prepare for controlled shutdown if vibration continues increasing.

The pump must be stopped immediately if vibration is accompanied by metallic noise, coupling impact, visible baseplate movement, smoke or rapid temperature increase. The technician must not attempt mechanical adjustment while the pump is running.

Possible causes include coupling misalignment, bearing damage, cavitation, impeller imbalance, loose foundation bolts or operation outside the recommended flow range.

## 4. Overheating

If overheating is detected, the technician must verify motor winding temperature, bearing temperature, cooling system condition, ambient temperature, motor current, recent load changes and ventilation obstruction.

If the temperature exceeds the alarm threshold but remains below the trip threshold, the shift supervisor must be notified and the pump must be monitored continuously. If the temperature reaches the trip threshold, the pump must be stopped according to the emergency shutdown procedure.

The pump must not be restarted until the cause of overheating has been identified and corrected.

## 5. Emergency Shutdown

Emergency shutdown is required when continued operation may create risk to personnel, equipment or process stability. The pump must be stopped immediately when smoke, burning smell, severe vibration, leakage near energized equipment, bearing temperature above trip limit, protection relay trip, loss of cooling, fire alarm or instruction from the shift supervisor occurs.

Emergency shutdown steps: press the local emergency stop if immediate action is required, confirm pump stop status, notify the shift supervisor, isolate affected equipment if required, place warning tags, record the event and do not restart until authorization is received.

## 6. Post-Maintenance Validation

After maintenance work, the technician must complete a validation run before returning the pump to normal service. The validation run must include visual inspection, valve position confirmation, motor current measurement, vibration measurement, bearing temperature monitoring, cooling verification, leak inspection and alarm clearance.

The pump must run for at least 30 minutes under stable operating conditions unless otherwise instructed by the reliability engineer. The task can be closed only when no abnormal vibration is detected, temperatures remain normal, no leaks are observed, current is stable and the shift supervisor accepts the equipment back into service.
